About

I am currently finishing up a PhD thesis at HU Berlin and expect to receive my degree soon from the department of physics! The essence of my work took place at the Paul-Drude-Institut under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Henning Riechert and Dr. Jens Herfort.

“How can physics help build the electronics of tomorrow?”— that's the question that motivates me. My master's thesis describes my first contributions to this cause. A general overview of my research can be found here.

My CV in a nutshell: an academia-oriented career path focused on the physics of new materials and their applications for quantum information technology. My scientific skills can be used to tackle broader analytical and technological challenges in the industry.

While studying with Guillaume Gervais, I was introduced to Python by some ingenious fellow graduate students who created this amazing LabGUI project. I've since acquired a penchant for programming in general.

Besides research, I enjoy epistemology, hosting dinners, and riding my bike on the Tempelhofer Feld. I write on this blog once in a while.
