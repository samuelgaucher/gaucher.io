About

I'm a PhD candidate in physics enrolled at Humboldt in Berlin. The essence of my work takes place at the Paul-Drude-Institut under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Henning Riechert and Dr. Jens Herfort.

How can physics help build the electronics of tomorrow? That's the question that motivates me. My master's thesis describes my first contributions to the cause. A general overview of my research can be found here.

My CV in a nutshell: an academia-oriented career path focused on the physics of new materials and their applications for quantum information technology.

While working for Guillaume Gervais, I was introduced to programming by some ingenious fellow graduate students. I'm now a committed user and advocate of their Python LabGUI project, which I cannot recommend highly enough.

Besides research, I enjoy epistemology, hosting dinners, and riding my bike on the Tempelhofer Feld. I will write on this blog once in a while.
