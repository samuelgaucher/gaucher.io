A pleasing LaTeX novel template: meet Pleanote

March 2019  |  Berlin


  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn

LaTeX is a powerful free tool to produce nice-looking documents. I have a passion for typesetting since I first learned how to use it — and I'm not the only one. LaTeX benefits from a vast community of users contributing with packages, tips, and of course, ready-made templates for all sorts of purposes.

Here's another contribution, this one meant to disrupt the world of online self-publishing. The Pleanote novel template:




  • pleanote.zip ZIP

  • GitHubZIP

Use Pleanote as you want by downloading the source code and replacing the text by your own. It is one out of many already existing great novel templates available online (Typesetters, ShareLaTeX.com, Daniel Grady). If you don't know how to use LaTeX, this guide can help you getting started.

The text featured in the document above was “written” with the kantlipsum package which uses the Kant Generator for Python created by Mark Pilgrim, a context-free grammar tool that outputs sentences in the style of renown German philosopher Immanuel Kant (which I find is a cool alternative to the common lipsum package).
